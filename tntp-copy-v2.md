WHY THIS, WHY NOW
EYEBROW
Why this, why now
PULL QUOTE (SERIF ITALIC)
"I liked school. I just never knew what I was preparing for."
DISPLAY STAT
38 minutes.
The average amount of real advising a student receives in a year of high school.
BODY PARAGRAPHS
A senior said that to us last year, two weeks before graduation. She had a 3.6 GPA, a seat at a four-year college, and no clear sense of why. She is not an outlier. Only 37 percent of middle and high schoolers say their coursework is connected to the real world. Only 21 percent of teachers say their college-bound students are very prepared. For students heading straight to the workforce, it's 10 percent.
While many districts have developed a portrait of a graduate, fewer have changed how they teach and measure those durable skills. Transforming advisory, or a similar space, is the spine that belonging, durable skills, and career readiness build on.
Done well, it is also where purpose gets built. Not in a single reflection or career quiz. Over years, through repeated cycles of exploration and feedback, with an advisor who actually knows the student. That's the bet.
And the field deserves better evidence on whether it's working. Advisory has not been studied the way core instruction has. The co-build is designed to produce real learning science on what belonging, purpose-building, and career readiness actually take inside a secondary school day, so what we learn travels beyond the cohort.
SOURCE LINE (MICRO)
Sources: Walton Family Foundation / Gallup 2025 Gen Z Study; Walton Family Foundation / RAND 2024 Teacher Survey.

WHAT OPPORTUNITY ADVISING IS
EYEBROW
The model
H2
What Opportunity Advising is.
BODY PARAGRAPHS (LEFT COLUMN)
A purpose-led, curriculum-led approach to advisory. Treated with the same seriousness as a core academic class. Sequenced, high-quality content. Consistent routines. Protected time. Measured outcomes. A coherent connection to the rest of the student's school experience.
Grounded in TNTP's Paths of Opportunity and Opportunity Makers research. TNTP's Opportunity Makers research identified 1,300 trajectory-changing schools where students who start behind catch up at dramatically higher rates. All three share the same qualities: belonging, coherence, and consistency. Advisory is where those three get built, or don't.
Delivered through a hybrid advising platform that unlocks the human relationship rather than replacing it.
CARD 01 — BELONGING BY DESIGN
Every student is known by multiple adults.
Belonging as a structural property of the school. Strengths, story, and aspirations documented and shared. Relationships that last across grades.
CARD 02 — CONSISTENCY
A shared curriculum, not just a shared intent.
Every advisor works from the same high-quality scope and sequence. Purpose-building, career exploration, and postsecondary planning show up on a schedule, not when an advisor has time to wing it.
CARD 03 — COHERENCE
Advising is the connective tissue.
Links core academics, career-connected learning, CTE, and postsecondary pathways into one student experience. Same advisor, same playbook, same direction.
CARD 04 — AI FOR HUMAN POTENTIAL
Human relationships first. AI supports.
Advisors use AI to prepare, prioritize, and personalize. Students use it to reflect, create, and decide. The technology unlocks the relationship. It does not replace it.

WHAT STUDENTS BUILD
EYEBROW
What students build
H3
Four outcomes. Measurable over time.
Transferable capability — the skills to apply what they learn in new contexts, again and again.
Social capital — relationships they can mobilize for opportunity, not just collect.
Navigation capacity — goal-setting, decision-making, help-seeking, and follow-through.
AI fluency — using AI to think, reflect, create, and decide. Aligned to OECD literacy and fluency standards.

WHY THIS IS DIFFERENT — DARK BLOCK
Eyebrow: Why this is different
The opposite starting place.
Too many systems think about career readiness as a platform to deploy. We think about it as the experiences, relationships, and durable skills students need while they explore who they are and where they're headed. Purpose first. Curriculum and advisor practice next. The platform is the last mile, not the starting line.
CALLOUT 1
Use the block your district already has.
Thirty minutes a week is enough to start. We're not asking you to add a period. We're asking you to take the block your district already protects and treat it like a core class.
CALLOUT 2
Every student. Not just some.
We serve all students. Not just the college-bound. Not just the at-risk. Not just the ones who already know what they want. Advisory is the one structure that reliably reaches every one of them.
CALLOUT 3
Quality and ROI drive every path.
College, apprenticeship, workforce, military, entrepreneurship. Students evaluate each one by completion rates and labor-market ROI, not by default or by who in their life went where.

HOW THE CO-BUILD WORKS
EYEBROW
The co-build
H2
You're not a pilot site. You're a co-builder.
BODY
A pilot says: we built the thing, try it. A co-build says: we have a framework, a curriculum, and a platform. Come help us finish building it for the real conditions in real districts. Selected districts get a seat at the design table, subgrant funding, and 18 months of deep support.
CARD 01 — CONVENE
Two 2-day in-person convenings.
One in fall (mid to late September 2026) to launch the co-build. One in spring (2027) to synthesize and plan scale. Bring a team of two or three district leaders to each.
CARD 02 — WORK
Monthly virtual workshops.
Between convenings, cohort districts meet monthly to solve real problems, refine routines, and share artifacts. Workshops are working sessions, not webinars.
CARD 03 — PILOT
Pilot with real students.
Districts run Opportunity Advising in selected schools and grade levels during the 2026–27 school year. Live classrooms, real data, honest reflection.

LEARNING AGENDA
EYEBROW
What we'll learn together
H3
Four questions. One cohort. Real answers.
• Does a structured, relationship-focused advisory curriculum produce a greater sense of belonging and clearer career identity?
• How can AI-enabled tools increase the ROI of human advising by automating administrative work and surfacing real-time student need?
• How can districts align advisory with their Portrait of a Graduate and career-connected learning to create one coherent student experience?
• Does early purpose-setting and AI-supported navigation lead to higher enrollment and persistence in positive-ROI postsecondary pathways?

FAQ
EYEBROW
Common questions
H2
Before you apply.
Q. Do we need to create a new period in our master schedule?
No. Opportunity Advising is designed to transform the time most districts already protect. Advisory, homeroom, seminar, mentor block. If you don't have a protected block today, we'll help you design one into the schedule.
Q. Do we need to adopt Willow's platform to participate?
Cohort districts use the Opportunity Advising platform and curriculum during the co-build period — hybrid advising, human-led and AI-supported. Both are included at no additional cost. We're looking for districts who want to build with us, and the platform is one of the things we're refining together.
Q. Is $50,000 enough to fund this work?
The subgrant is designed to cover the extra cost of participating: backfill time for design team educators, planning days, data collection, travel to convenings. The core implementation support, curriculum, and platform access are provided in addition to the subgrant.
Q. What happens after the 18 months?
Cohort districts have a clear path to continued partnership on standard commercial terms. Many will become flagship sites for the scaled offer. Districts are not locked in, and nothing about the cohort requires a multi-year commercial commitment on the other side.
Q. Who do we contact with questions before applying?
Email cobuild@opportunityadvising.org. We're happy to jump on a 20-minute call with district teams weighing whether to apply.
